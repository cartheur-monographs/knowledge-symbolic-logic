# Journal Shortlist

Prepared: July 17, 2026

## Best current fit

### 1. Minds and Machines

Publisher:
Springer Nature

Why it fits:
This is the strongest fit for the paper in its current form. The journal describes itself as a forum for scientifically grounded conceptual analyses at the intersection of artificial intelligence, philosophy, cognitive science, logic, epistemology, ethics, and computer/data science. That matches the paper's present mix of symbolic logic, machine architecture, embodied intelligence, and conceptual comparison between Berkeley and Heiserman.

What would help before submission:
- Keep the argumentative and conceptual core strong.
- Make the contribution to current AI theory explicit in the introduction and conclusion.
- Tighten the novelty claim around architecture, embodiment, and intelligent-machine design.

Preprint policy:
Springer Nature states that posting preprints does not count as prior publication and will not jeopardize consideration at its journals.

Current recommendation:
`Top journal choice for the paper as it exists now.`

Official sources:
- Journal page: https://link.springer.com/journal/11023
- Springer Nature preprint policy: https://www.springer.com/de/editorial-policies/preprint-sharing

### 2. Adaptive Behavior

Publisher:
SAGE

Why it fits:
This is the best fit if we continue revising the paper toward embodied, adaptive, robotic, and behavioral intelligence. The journal explicitly positions itself around complex adaptive systems, robotic and computational investigations of behavior and cognition, and theoretical developments. It is a good home if we want Berkeley and Heiserman to be received as contributors to a live embodied-AI research program rather than mainly as figures in conceptual history.

What would help before submission:
- Strengthen the adaptive-behavior framing further.
- Expand the comparative discussion with Heiserman and later behavior-based robotics.
- Add one more explicit section on how the Berkeley-Heiserman line could inform present-day embodied-agent design.

Preprint policy:
The journal states that it accepts preprints and will consider manuscripts posted on preprint servers.

Current recommendation:
`Best choice if the next revision becomes more explicitly embodied and behavior-theoretic.`

Official sources:
- Journal page: https://uk.sagepub.com/en-gb/eur/journal/adaptive-behavior
- Submission guidelines: https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/adb

### 3. Robotics and Autonomous Systems

Publisher:
Elsevier

Why it fits:
This is the strongest engineering-side option. The journal's aims include fundamental developments in robotics and autonomous systems, with explicit interest in both symbolic and sensory-based robot control and learning. That language is unusually sympathetic to the core thesis of this paper.

What would help before submission:
- Recast the paper more assertively as a robotics architecture paper.
- Add a clearer bridge from Berkeley and Heiserman to present-day robot control design.
- Consider adding a concrete research agenda or prototype architecture section.

Preprint policy:
The guide for authors says authors may share preprints and that preprint sharing will not count as prior publication.

Current recommendation:
`Good option only if we push the manuscript further toward engineering and robotics architecture.`

Official sources:
- Journal page: https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/robotics-and-autonomous-systems
- Guide for authors: https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/robotics-and-autonomous-systems/publish/guide-for-authors

### 4. AI & Society

Publisher:
Springer Nature

Why it fits:
This journal is a plausible destination if we want the paper to land as an intervention in how AI should be conceived and designed, especially against current trends that narrow AI to statistical optimization. Its scope is broad, interdisciplinary, and explicitly concerned with philosophical, cognitive, ethical, and societal implications of AI and digital systems.

What would help before submission:
- Make the significance for present and future AI design more explicit.
- Add a stronger section on why this architecture matters now, beyond historical recovery.
- Possibly expand the discussion of current AI assumptions that the paper challenges.

Preprint policy:
As a Springer Nature journal, it falls under Springer Nature's policy that preprints do not count as prior publication.

Current recommendation:
`Strong alternative if we want wider AI discourse reach rather than a narrower architecture or behavior journal.`

Official sources:
- Journal page: https://link.springer.com/journal/146
- Springer Nature preprint policy: https://www.springer.com/de/editorial-policies/preprint-sharing

## Recommended path

If we freeze the paper near its current balance:
`Submit to Minds and Machines after arXiv.`

If we deepen the embodied-behavior and adaptive-architecture direction:
`Submit to Adaptive Behavior after arXiv.`

If we rewrite more aggressively toward robotics engineering:
`Consider Robotics and Autonomous Systems.`

If we want the paper read as a larger intervention in AI futures and design culture:
`Consider AI & Society.`
