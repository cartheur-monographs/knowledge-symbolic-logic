# arXiv Submission Notes

Prepared: July 17, 2026

## Recommended category

Primary: `cs.AI`

Reason:
This paper argues for a still-live architecture of machine intelligence centered on logic, control, state organization, and embodied behavior. That fits arXiv's `cs.AI` description, which covers AI broadly outside the separate robotics and machine learning areas, including knowledge representation and planning.

Secondary: `cs.RO`

Reason:
The paper is substantially about robots, embodied control, and behavior-organizing machine architectures, even though it is not an experimental robotics paper.

Optional alternative secondary:
`cs.CY` only if the paper is revised further toward the societal implications of AI rather than the architecture of intelligent systems themselves.

## Recommended license

Safest default for later journal submission:
`arXiv.org perpetual, non-exclusive license 1.0`

Why:
This keeps the preprint openly available on arXiv while minimizing reuse complications for later journal publication.

Alternative:
`CC BY 4.0` if the authors want broad reuse rights and the target journal clearly permits preprints under that license.

## What arXiv currently requires

- Submission should be uploaded as source, not just the compiled PDF, when the manuscript is written in LaTeX.
- arXiv prefers `(La)TeX, AMS(La)TeX, PDFLaTeX`.
- arXiv does not accept PDF created from TeX/LaTeX source as the submitted format.
- If the submitter is new to arXiv or new to the chosen category, endorsement may be required.
- The chosen license is irrevocable for that version.
